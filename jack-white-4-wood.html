The provenance

The man Bobby Jones trusted with his driver.

This isn't a replica or a tribute. It's a fairway wood built around a real clubhead from the workshop of one of the early game's most respected hands.

Sunningdale's first head pro. The 1904 Open Champion.

Jack White took the head professional post at Sunningdale in 1901, then won The Open at Royal St George's in 1904. But it was at the bench, not just on the links, that he made his name. A clubmaker whose work outlasted his playing record.

"Bobby Jones played a Jack White-built driver his whole career. He called it Jeanie Deans."

When the greatest amateur the game has known reaches for your work first, that's a reputation you can hold in your hands. This 4 wood carries the same maker's mark.

Retro-fit means it's built to be played, not babied.

We paired the original head with a fresh, stiff hickory shaft and set it to a true D5 swing weight. The result is a 41½-inch, 20-degree fairway wood that comes off the deck low and hot, with the heft you feel through impact and the kind of feedback a hickory league rewards.

Hit it off the deck on a links afternoon. Then hang it on the wall when you're done. Either way, you own a piece nobody else can. There is exactly one.
